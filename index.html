The Problem

People keep getting stuck before they start.

All over the UK, people want to start a business. They are smart. They work hard. They have good ideas. Some have degrees. Some have years of experience. They all believe they could build something.

But most of them never start. They read books. They watch videos. They take courses. Then they go back to their normal life and nothing changes. They keep going back and forth between feeling excited and doing nothing.

They do not need more information. They need a step-by-step plan and someone to hold them to it.

"The fear of losing money, wasting time, and having to tell people it did not work out keeps them stuck."
— Why most people never start
How We Do It

We build businesses, not just knowledge.

We found seven things that stop first-time founders from getting their first paying client. Every programme we run is built to fix each one.

A Clear Plan

We cover what matters: Mindset, Offers, Sales, Marketing, and Operations. Every session ends with something you can use right away — not just notes to look at later.

Real Accountability

Daily check-ins, tracked progress, live sessions, and honest feedback. Our programmes are made for busy people. The work happens in short, focused bursts — not endless study.

Real Results

You build your actual business inside the programme. You test your offer. You get your first clients. You make money. The goal is a running business, not a business plan.

Our Impact

We go where the need is greatest.

We built Mind and Method Academy for the people that most business education ignores: people starting from scratch. People without money, without connections, without anyone in their life who has done it before.

Our founder has run workshops in universities, community centres, schools, and prisons across three continents. Every programme is built around reality — people who are busy, who are careful with money, and who need a clear path they can follow in short bursts.

We measure success one way: did they build a business? Everything else is secondary.
